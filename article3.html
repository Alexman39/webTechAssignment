From Customer Support to Code: My Unexpected Tech Journey

Before I became a developer, I spent three years working in customer support. While it might seem unrelated, that experience was invaluable in sharpening my communication skills and shaping how I approach problem-solving in tech.

In this article, I share how interacting with diverse customers taught me empathy, patience, and the ability to break down complex issues into simple explanations — skills that make me a better programmer and teammate today.

If you’re coming from a non-technical background or wondering how your previous experience can translate into a tech career, this article provides a hopeful and practical perspective on making that leap.
