Why I Started Building a Fitness App as My First Web Project

When I first began coding, I knew I wanted my first real project to be something personal, something I’d stick with. As someone passionate about both fitness and technology, it made perfect sense to combine the two. That’s how I started building a fitness tracking app — not just to learn, but to create something truly useful.

This project gave me more than just practice with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. It introduced me to:

  • Designing for real users
  • Debugging complex user interactions
  • Creating intuitive interfaces
  • Managing files and folders structures properly

What I Learned Along the Way

Instead of working on a generic website, I dove into the needs of people like me — those who wanted to log workouts, track progress, and stay accountable. Here are some core features I focused on:

Feature Priority User Benefit Status
Workout Logger High Tracks daily exercises Completed
Progress Dashboard Medium Visualizes user improvement In Progress
Nutrition Tracker Low Logs calories/macros Planned
Mobile Responsiveness High Improves usability on phones Completed
